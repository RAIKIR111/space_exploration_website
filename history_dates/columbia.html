April 12, 1981 - launch of the reusable spacecraft, Columbia. NASA's space shuttle was the world's first reusable spacecraft. It launched like a rocket and returned to Earth like a glider. It was designed to carry large payloads — such as satellites — into orbit and bring them back, if necessary, for repairs.
