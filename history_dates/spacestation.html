April 19, 1971 - launch of the first Soviet space station, Salyut 1. The Salyut program followed this with five more successful launches of seven more stations. The final module of the program, Zvezda (DOS-8) became the core of the Russian segment of the International Space Station and remains in orbit.

  • Mass - 18,425 kg (40,620 lb);
  • Length ~20 m (66 ft);
  • Diameter ~4 m (13 ft);
  • Days in orbit - 175 days;
